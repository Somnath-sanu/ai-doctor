# Design System: MEDIVA AI
**Project ID:** Local medical workspace

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
MEDIVA AI should feel like a premium clinical command center rather than a generic SaaS dashboard. The atmosphere is calm, precise, and protective: soft luminous surfaces in light mode, deep inky layers in dark mode, and restrained high-contrast accents that suggest trust instead of hype. The overall mood is polished, modern, and medically credible, with a quiet sense of intelligence.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
- Arctic Pearl (#F6F8FB): Primary page canvas in light mode.
- Clouded Slate (#E8EEF5): Soft section tint and elevated separators in light mode.
- Ink Harbor (#0F1720): Primary dark canvas and deep shell background.
- Night Glass (#16212E): Elevated dark surfaces and glassy panels.
- Clinical Teal (#1D8A8A): Primary action color, active states, and trusted highlights.
- Ocean Signal (#0F6CBD): Secondary accent for links, outlines, and badges.
- Warm Coral (#D96C4A): Attention color for urgency and destructive actions without harsh neon intensity.
- Soft Fern (#4C9B6C): Success color for completed workflows and validated states.
- Mist Text (#5E6C7B): Secondary body copy in light mode.
- Frost Text (#C8D3DE): Secondary body copy in dark mode.

## 3. Typography Rules
- Headings use a confident modern grotesk with compact tracking and strong vertical rhythm.
- Section titles should feel editorial and deliberate, not oversized or heavy-handed.
- Body text should remain highly legible with moderate line-height and slightly softened color contrast.
- Serif usage is reserved for report content and long-form clinical summaries to create a document-like reading mode.
- Monospace is used sparingly for metadata, timestamps, and compact technical labels.

## 4. Component Stylings
- **Buttons:** Pill-leaning silhouettes with confident padding, strong hover feedback, and obvious pointer affordance. Primary buttons should feel like polished controls, not flat default rectangles.
- **Cards/Containers:** Generously rounded corners, layered borders, and whisper-soft glow or depth. Cards should look elevated from the background without appearing noisy.
- **Inputs/Forms:** Softly inset surfaces with clear placeholder readability, visible focus rings, and enough contrast in both light and dark themes.
- **Sidebars:** Tinted shell panels with subtle translucency, active rows that feel illuminated, and iconography that remains readable in collapsed mode.
- **Dialogs:** Large, document-like reading surfaces with strong hierarchy, readable content widths, and visible separation from the app shell.

## 5. Layout Principles
- Use wide breathing space at the page level, but keep internal cards dense enough for workflow efficiency.
- Balance ambient backgrounds with calm solid surfaces so text always remains readable.
- Favor grouped content blocks with clear sectional rhythm over long undifferentiated stacks.
- Desktop layouts should feel spacious and premium; mobile layouts should keep the same tone while collapsing into clean vertical flows.
